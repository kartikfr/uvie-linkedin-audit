One-Line Diagnosis

UVIE has the posting cadence, the follower base, and the subject matter to be a leading voice in India's renewable energy space. The frequency is there. What is missing is the system behind it — a unified visual identity, a consistent brand voice, and a CTA strategy that turns impressions into conversations.

What's Not Working

Six issues holding the page back from its potential

🎨

No Visual Design System — 6 Styles Running at Once

Scroll UVIE's feed and it looks like posts from five different companies. Dark gradient tech, blue wave corporate, raw photo overlays, soft pastel festive templates, and a cinematic Hindi creative with fire effects — none of them connected. The problem is not that any single post looks bad. It is that as a feed, there is no visual thread. A visitor cannot build a brand impression because nothing is consistent enough to hold onto.

Fix

One design system: 1 heading font, 1 body font, 3 brand colors, 4 reusable templates covering Education, Hiring, Event, and Employee posts.

🗺

3 Different Logos Still in Circulation

  • Current UVIE logo — "Integrated Energy"
  • Older variant — "Infinite Possibilities"
  • Circular ultraVIBRANT legacy burst logo

Three active logos signal a company mid-rebrand that has not finished the job. Every time a different mark appears, it dilutes the brand.

Fix

Retire all legacy variants immediately. One logo, everywhere, no exceptions.

🔖

Banner and About Section are Wasted Space

The banner uses a stock dark circuit graphic with no connection to solar work, no CTA, and copy that could describe any tech company. The About section opens with UVIE's naming history instead of what they actually deliver for clients.

Fix

Banner: real project photo + "India's Trusted Solar EPC + BESS Partner" + contact CTA. About: open with MW delivered and client outcomes, not company history.

✍️

Captions Have No Hook and No CTA

Most captions trail off before the "see more" cut. There is no hook-insight-CTA structure. Most posts end without asking the audience to do anything — no link, no question, no prompt. Brand voice also flips between formal corporate, casual consumer, and Hindi vernacular with no system.

Every caption should follow this structure
1
Hook — 1 bold line that earns the click to "see more"
2
Context — 2 lines setting up why this matters
3
Insight — 2 lines of the actual value or perspective
4
CTA — 1 direct ask: comment, DM, visit link, or tag someone
5
Hashtags — 5 fixed: #UVIE #UltravibrantEnergy #SolarEPC #BESS #RenewableIndia
📈

Content Mix is Built for a Job Board, Not a B2B Brand

Hiring posts (25%) and festive greetings (20%) together account for nearly half of all content. At 9,223 followers, the page has an audience that wants to see UVIE's expertise — what they build, how they think, what they know about India's energy transition. The current mix does not deliver that.

Brand Name Mismatch Creates Trust Friction

The LinkedIn page promotes UVIE / Ultravibrant Integrated Energy, but every creative points to vibrantsolar.in. A new visitor — particularly a procurement decision-maker — has no way to know these are the same company. This creates doubt at exactly the moment they are deciding whether to reach out.

Fix

Add one line in the About section: "Solutions delivered under the Vibrant Solar brand — vibrantsolar.in" Or update to a UVIE-branded domain.

🎥

Video Content is Underperforming

The EPC explainer and industrial solar videos are getting 17–18 likes, well below the page average of ~45. LinkedIn videos autoplay without sound. Without a bold text hook in the caption and a strong first frame that tells the story before play, viewers scroll past before the content lands.

Fix

Add subtitles to all videos. Write a 1-line bold hook at the top of every video caption. Design a first frame that communicates the message before the viewer presses play.
